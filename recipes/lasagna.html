Lasagna

Delicious mouth watering lasagna

Making lasagna is time consuming but the results are worth the process.This recipe is great for feeding a big family and freeze well, too

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive olive
  • 500g minced beef
  • 1 onion,2 cloves Garlic, 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • 1 tbsp tomato puree
  • 4 medium fresh chopped tomatoes
  • 9-12 coocked lasagna sheets
  • 1 Egg to help bind ricotta
  • 2tbs cube beef stock
  • 50g Parmesan grated
  • 150 mozzarella shredded
  • parsely
  • dried basil leaves
  • italian Seasoning
  • fennel seeds
  • black pepper
  • Salt
For white sauce
  • butter
  • plain flour
  • 55ml semi-skimmed milk

Steps for making lasagna

  1. In a large pan, heat the olive oil over a low heat. Fry the onion,garlic and ginger for 5 mins, or until softened. Add the mince and fry on a medium heat until golden. Turn up the heat. Stir in the tomato purée, chopped tomatoes,seasonings and stock cubes.Simmer for 15 munites until the tomatoes are cooked.
  2. Meanwhile, make the white sauce. Melt the butter in a small saucepan over a low heat and add the flour. Whisk until combined and cook on low for 1-2 mins. Remove from the heat and gradually whisk in the milk until you have a loose sauce. Season with a pinch of salt and black pepper. Return to a gentle heat and whisk constantly until the sauce thickens.
  3. Preheat the oven to gas 6, 200°C, fan 180°C. Layer up the lasagne in a baking dish, starting with a third each of the ragu(cooked minced meat and tomato sauce),then the pasta, then the white sauce. Repeat twice. Top with the Parmesan and mozzarella then bake in the oven for 40-45 mins, until piping hot and crisp and bubbling on top. Serve immediately.
Freezing and defrosting guidelines

Once the dish has cooled completely, transfer it to an airtight, freezer-safe container, seal and freeze for up to 1-3 months. To serve, defrost thoroughly in the fridge overnight before reheating. Loosely cover with foil and bake until dish is thoroughly heated through. Reheat until piping hot.
